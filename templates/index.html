What is Only Connect?

Introduced to the world by the British Broadcasting Corporation in 2008, Only Connect is often cited as one of television's hardest quiz shows. Host Victoria Coren-Mitchell pits two teams of three against each other in a tournament-style competition of finding the connection between seemingly unrelated clues, forcing contestants to use lateral thinking and patience to find the answers that will get them the points they need to win the game.

The game is divided into four rounds:

  1. Teams must find the connection between four seemingly random clues. The less clues teams see before finding the connection, the more points they will get for solving the puzzle. If the team provides an incorrect answer or fails to buzz in before the time is up, the other team has a chance to steal the puzzle and receive a point.
  2. Each set of four clues in this round makes up a sequence. The teams are only allowed to see a maximum of three clues, and must provide what they would expect to see in the fourth clue as their solution. Again, the less clues teams see before providing the solution, the more points that they will get for solving the puzzle. If the team provides an incorrect answer or fails to buzz in before the time is up, the other team has a chance to steal the puzzle and receive a point.
  3. The connecting wall, which consists of 16 randomized clues. These clues can be grouped into four groups of four clues such that each clue is used exactly once, and teams are given a 2 minute and 30 second time limit to group them all into the four distinct groups, earning a point for each correct group successfully formed. After the clues are grouped, teams then must provide the connection for each set of clues, earning a point for each correct connection provided. If all the groups and connections are found, the team receives a two point bonus for a maximum of 10 points able to be earned in this round.
  4. The missing vowels round provides teams with an overarching category and an answer that falls under that category. However, the answer provided has had all vowels from the solution removed and the spacing rearranged. The teams are actively competing against each other in this round and must buzz in when they know the answer. If they are correct, they receive a point; however, if they are incorrect, even by one letter, they actually lose a point and give the opposing team the chance to steal and gain a point.

At the end of four rounds, the team with the most points is the winner! If there is a tie, a tiebreaker missing vowels puzzle is played with the captains of each team where a correct answer wins the game and an incorrect answer loses the game.

What is the Only Connect Host Assistant?

If you've seen an episode of Only Connect and thought "Hey, I can totally be Victoria Coren-Mitchell!", the Only Connect Host Assistant is for you! Simply download this template and insert your clues and connections inside the empty double quotes. Once you've completely filled out the template, save it under a new name and upload that new file at the top of this page; if successful, you will be able to start choosing team names and running the game!

Note: The Only Connect Host Assistant has been modified slightly from the traditional Only Connect game in some ways, including:

  • The application is only able to accept regular text based clues at this time. Picture and music clues will (hopefully) be coming at a later time. The field "type" is included in the question template to allow for future functionality.
  • The time limits of the traditional rounds of Only Connect have been modified slightly.
    • In rounds 1 and 2, teams are given 45 seconds instead of 40 seconds to answer the questions.
    • In round 3, teams are given 180 seconds instead of 150 seconds to solve the Connecting Wall.
    • In round 4, no time limit for the round or each question is enforced by the application. The host is tasked with providing four missing vowel round categories with four clues in each (a total of 16 puzzles) for the duration of the round. Each question does not have a strict time limit, but the host may at their own discretion reveal the answer of the puzzle and skip to the next one without causing any point changes as in the show.
